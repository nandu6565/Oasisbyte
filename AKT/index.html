ABDUL KALAM

(1931-2015)

About

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and is widely regarded as the "People's President" due to his humble and inspiring personality.

Dr. Kalam played a key role in the development of India's space and missile programs. He also made significant contributions to the field of science and technology and was known for his vision of making India a developed nation by 2020.

Apart from his scientific and political achievements, Dr. Abdul Kalam was an excellent orator and writer. He wrote several books, including his autobiography "Wings of Fire," which became immensely popular and inspired millions of people.

Dr. Kalam was a true inspiration to the youth of India and the world. His dedication, integrity, and positive attitude continue to motivate people to strive for excellence and make a difference in society.
